About Us

The Department of Land Administration and Management, under the Ministry of Lands, Agriculture, Fisheries, Water and Rural Development, is responsible for managing land resources and implementing land reform programs in Zimbabwe. Our mission is to facilitate equitable access to land and sustainable land use for social and economic development.

With our system, we can simplify public access to land records by maintaining digitally accurate and up-to-date information for the land in your area. Our GIS tools provide a platform for modeling, visualization, collaboration, and analysis. With multiple criteria evaluation (MCE), land management becomes an interactive task, allowing for the weighting of various criteria based on importance.

Knowledge Center

map data

Our land management system integrates with map data to provide a powerful tool for land governance. GIS connects data to a map, integrating location data (where things are) with all types of descriptive information (what things are like there). Our system allows for the integration and collective analysis of geospatial data from multiple sources, including satellite imagery, GPS recordings, and textual attributes associated with a particular space. GIS components include visualizations through interactive maps and data based on the location of features or variables represented.

Farm Register

Our land management system can also integrate with farm registers that contain information about farm owners. With our system, farmers with accounts can access their farm records and maps online. This allows them to view, print, and export detailed farm records such as cropland, base acres, yields, CRP acres, land ownership details, and much more. They can also view, print, and export farm/tract maps that can be provided to lenders, chemical or fertilizer providers, and FSA for reporting acreage and crop insurance agents. The ability to access these records on demand without a visit to the service center saves farmers time and money.

Ressetlement

Farm resettlement refers to the process of relocating farmers from their original farmland to a new location. This can happen for various reasons such as urbanization, land expropriation, and policy changes. If you want to know more about this process go to the knowledge center.
